FeedCMU

An innovative web-based platform designed to enhance food accessibility and distribution at Carnegie Mellon University.

FeedCMU Project Overview

FeedCMU is a web-based application developed to address food insecurity among students at Carnegie Mellon University. By leveraging technology, FeedCMU connects students in need with surplus food from campus dining facilities, local businesses, and events. The platform serves as a bridge, ensuring that food that would otherwise go to waste is redirected to those who need it most.

The application utilizes a real-time database to track available food items and their locations, enabling students to quickly find and claim food. FeedCMU also incorporates notifications to alert students when new food items become available, ensuring timely and efficient distribution.

Key Features:

  • Real-Time Food Tracking: The platform tracks available food items in real time, providing up-to-date information to users.
  • User Notifications: Students receive instant notifications when new food items are added, allowing them to act quickly.
  • Interactive Map: An integrated map helps users locate food pickup points across the campus.
  • Community Integration: Partners with local businesses and campus events to maximize food distribution opportunities.

Technology Stack:

  • Frontend: React.js for a responsive and interactive user interface.
  • Backend: Node.js and Express.js for server-side logic and API management.
  • Database: Firebase real-time database to manage food item data and user information.
  • Notifications: Firebase Cloud Messaging for real-time notifications.

Impact and Future Plans:

  • Reducing Food Waste: By redistributing surplus food, FeedCMU significantly reduces food waste on campus.
  • Supporting Students: Provides a vital resource for students facing food insecurity, helping them focus on their studies.
  • Scalability: Plans to expand FeedCMU to other universities and communities, adapting the platform to different environments and needs.

Project Details

  • Technologies: React.js, Node.js, Express.js, Firebase
  • Date: November 2024
  • Category: Web Application, Social Impact
